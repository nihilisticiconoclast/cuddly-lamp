Chapter 9 · Markov Chain Monte Carlo

Sampling is a walk across a landscape you can’t see.

A chain doesn’t evaluate the posterior everywhere — it moves through it, the way a walker crosses terrain. The hard regions are barriers; the typical set is the valley floor; tuning is just learning the line through the pass.

The barrier, drawn two ways

On a map, the dashed line through the ridge is a tunnel: the path goes through the high ground, not over it. In quantum mechanics the same figure describes an amplitude that decays as ψ ∝ e^(−κx) inside the wall and re-emerges on the far side. The wider the barrier, the steeper the decay and the smaller what survives — which is exactly the intuition for why a poorly-tuned sampler stalls at a region of low probability mass.
