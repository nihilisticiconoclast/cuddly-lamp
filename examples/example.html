Statistical Rethinking · ways-to-produce

One black face is showing. What are the odds the other side is black?

Three cards in a bag: one black on both sides, one black on one side and white on the other, one white on both. You draw one at random, drop it on the table, and the face you can see is black. The intuition says one-half. The intuition is wrong.

Count the ways, not the cards

The trap is conditioning on the card when you should condition on the face. A black face you could be looking at has several equally likely origins — so count those, not the three cards.

cardways to show a black face
black / black2
black / white1
white / white0

Three ways produce the black face you’re seeing, and two of them belong to the black/black card — whose reverse is also black. So the answer is 2 / 3, not one-half. This is Bayes’ rule wearing work clothes: the posterior is just the count of paths consistent with the evidence, normalised.
